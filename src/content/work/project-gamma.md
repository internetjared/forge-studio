Park Collective was two years into doing remarkable residential architecture work with zero brand presence to match. Their portfolio existed only in a PDF they emailed to prospective clients. The work was exceptional. Nobody knew it existed.

## The Challenge

Architecture firms have a particular branding challenge: the work has to speak first. Any visual identity that overshadows the projects is a mistake. The identity had to be confident and distinctive without competing with what matters — the buildings.

The secondary challenge: Diana, the founder, had strong opinions about what she didn't want. She'd seen too many architecture firm websites that felt cold, corporate, and indistinguishable from each other. She wanted something that felt like it had a point of view.

## The Approach

The breakthrough came in the second discovery session, when Diana described her approach to residential architecture: "I design for how people actually live, not for how they want their house to look in a photo." That distinction — livability over aesthetics — became the positioning platform.

**Visual identity**: Warm off-white dominant with amber accent — not the stark black-and-white of most architecture firms. The typography used Cormorant Garamond for display headlines (editorial, slightly literary) with General Sans for body copy (approachable, readable).

**Site structure**: Portfolio-first design. The homepage leads immediately into project work, with the identity elements in service of the photography rather than competing with it. Full-bleed project images, generous white space, type that gets out of the way.

**Motion**: CSS-only approach — scroll-triggered reveals on project images, a subtle parallax on the hero. Nothing that drew attention to itself.

## The Result

The site launched on a Thursday. The first project inquiry arrived Saturday morning. Within sixty days, Dezeen featured one of their projects and the site handled the traffic spike without issue (Cloudflare Pages, zero configuration). They were booked out fourteen months within the first quarter — Diana eventually had to take the contact form down temporarily.
